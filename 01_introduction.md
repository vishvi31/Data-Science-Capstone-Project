## Introduction

SpaceX has revolutionised commercial space travel by making
rocket launches significantly cheaper than competitors.
The key to this cost reduction is the reuse of the Falcon 9
first stage booster, which lands back after launch.

**Cost comparison:**
- SpaceX Falcon 9 launch: $62 million
- Competitors: $165 million+

**Business Problem:**
If we can predict whether the first stage will successfully
land and be reused, we can estimate the cost of a launch.
This information is valuable for competing companies and
organisations planning satellite launches.

**Project Goal:**
Build a machine learning classification model that predicts
whether the Falcon 9 first stage will land successfully.

---

## Questions We Will Answer

1. What factors determine if the booster will land successfully?
2. How does payload mass affect landing success?
3. Which launch sites have the best success rates?
4. Which ML model best predicts landing success?
